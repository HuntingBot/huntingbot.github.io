B3/S238 (EightLife, Pulsar Life)

B3/S238 is a very close relative to Life. It is called EightLife because it is identical to Life, except for survival with 8 neighbors.

The still-lifes are identical to those in Life. As there are no new birth conditions and all the old survival conditions still exist, all old still-lifes continue to work. Any additional still-lifes would include cells with 8 living neighbors, but four of those cells would necessarily not be stable, so no new still-lifes can exist either. Honeycombs are much more common, as solitary tables that normally die in life evolve into honeycombs in EightLife.

Most Life oscillators work in EightLife, with the notable exception of Lonely Bees and similar oscillators, that rely on the table evolution. There are a few additional oscillators that do not work in Life.

Most Life spaceships work in EightLife, with the notable exceptions of the HWSS and OWSS and any other spaceships that involve similar mechanisms.

Many methuselahs evolve differently. In particular, the pi heptomino turns into a pulsar, making pulsars much more common in this rule. For this reason, this rule is also called Pulsar Life. Most dirty puffers also produce different exhaust than they do in Life.

LifeWiki: EightLife, Heavyweight spaceship, Overweight spaceship, Pulsar, Snacker.

Pulsar; Pulsar CP 48-54-72 [2]

Life’s pulsar works in EightLife, but because it evolves from the pi heptomino, it is much more common, and can be synthesized from 2 gliders.

The Pulsar consists of four inducting Traffic light predecessors. Its predecessors are used in many other oscillators (as described under Traffic light). Pulsars are also infinitely extensible, by arranging multiple quadrants in a closed loop. The Pulsar, consisting of four quadrants, is the smallest such arrangement. The Quasar, consisting of twelve quadrants, is the next largest.

1 Period 15 oscillator

– ⇧ ↑ ↓ ⇩ PNG RLE APG HRD LIS SOF ZIP SRCH

Pentadecathlon [3]

Life’s pentadecathlon also works in EightLife, but generations 2…4 evolve slightly differently in the interior.

The pentadecathlon consists of two forming Traffic light predecessors. It produces a useful domino spark, making it useful in hassler oscillators of many periods, including 15, 30, 45 60, and 120. Both its end and side sparks can eat gliders and the three simple spaceships in many different ways. Its end spark is also useful for reflecting gliders, and is used in glider shuttles of many periods, including 45, 60 and 75.

Its phase can also be altered (as described under the notes for Traffic light).

B38/S23 (Pedestrian Life)

B38/S23 is a very close relative to Life. It is called Pedestrian Life because the common traffic light predecessor dies rather than evolving into a traffic light, making the usually common traffic lights extremely rare. Pulsars are also much more rare, as they usually form from traffic-light predecessors in Life.

The still-lifes are identical to those in Life. As there are no new survival conditions and all the old birth conditions still exist, no new still-lifes will work. All Life still-lifes that would fail would include dead cells with 8 living neighbors, but four of those cells would necessarily not be stable, so no old still-lifes would be excluded either.

Most Life oscillators and spaceships work in Pedestrian Life, with the notable exception of all pre-pulsar shuttles, since those rely on the evolution of traffic lights. There are a few additional oscillators that do not work in Life. Many methuselahs evolve differently. Most dirty puffers also produce different exhaust than they do in Life.

LifeWiki: Pedestrian life, Pulsar, Traffic light.

B38/S238 (Honey Life)

B38/S238 is a very close relative to Life, sharing many features of EightLife and Pedestrian Life. It is called Honey Life because, as in EightLife, tables turn into honeycombs (12.121), making those very common.

The still-lifes are identical to those in Life, EightLife, and Pedestrian Life.

LifeWiki: Honey Life, Honeycomb.

Introduction

The rule B2n3/S23-q, also known as LeapLife, is similar to Life, but has some distinctive differences. All four of Life’s basic spaceships work. It also has Lepa, a natural (2,1)c/8 knightship. One distinctive characteristic is the B2n condition, which allows patterns to interact at greater distance, and makes many synthesis techniques difficult or impossible. This rule is less explosive than Life, and there are no known patterns with infinite chaotic growth; most small patterns become stable after at most a few hundred generations.
